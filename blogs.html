The Art of Just Starting

May 6, 2026

| "You don't have to have it all figured out. Just start"

We come across this quote quite often, while listening to motivational speakers or while scrolling through Instagram, but we really don't take it seriously. It's quite obvious—those two words “Just Start” might appear vague to many, like what do you even mean? How can I start? I don't even know the ABCs of this thing. But really, you don't need to have it all figured out, trust me. This quote is meant for people who struggle with taking action—it's quite normal nowadays, where people are more bothered about perfectionism, judgement, wanting to know it all, but reality is the opposite.

Start raw, start dirty

Perfectionism is procrastination in disguise. Nothing is ever perfect. When Zuckerberg started Facebook, he didn't start with a blueprint; he didn't start with a ton of money. He started in his dorm room with one small idea, with only a little funding. He started rough, and it was ugly. Let me frame it in his own words: “ideas don't come out fully formed” — you never start with a perfect plan, a perfect product, a perfect idea. You start it raw and dirty, and perfect is something that evolves over decades. Wanting something to be perfect before you start is something that keeps you from taking action. If Zuck had started off wanting to start Facebook as a perfect website, he wouldn't have ever started. Therefore, its ok to start dirty and build it up from there.

How do I start if I don't even know the basics?

This mindset is what stops you from taking action. You really don't need to know a thing before you begin, like literally, how would you even know a thing if you don't start? If you feel you must know it before you start, the majority of the time you're wrong. When I was in the process of building this website, I didn't have an idea about HTML and CSS, but I didn't let that stop me. I just picked up my laptop and just asked Claude; I didn't know how I was gonna do it, but I just took action, and 5 days later, I had a whole website that was live—it wasn't like I asked it to generate the website for me I coded it myself while It told me how to, and now if i had to make it all over again i could do it without asking. You really don't need to know before you start; you build knowledge over time after you make the first move.

How to start:

There is no best way to start, but I'll give you the simplest way to do so. The exact steps I followed when building this website: I started by pausing for a second, then I said the task out loud, quickly after this, I took the smallest action possible: for me, it was to pick up my laptop without planning or defining any goals. This initially set me in motion. But if it hadn't worked out, I would have added two more steps, first i would have set a very small goal that could have been done in less than 2 minutes, like asking Claude how to start building this website, and writing the first line of code. The final result of this would have motivated me right enough to keep going. This is valid for most of the things apart from coding, like studies, doing chores, etc.

  • The key here is not to complicate it, keep it simple, just start.

    • Finally, one last point: never fear failures, it's totally okay and normal to fail sometimes: you never learn from success, you learn from failures. They teach you what works and what doesn't, and even if you fail, your experience is worth way more than you think. Not everybody wins the first time, keep trying and you will win. JK. Rowling's Harry Potter was rejected multiple times before Bloomsbury said yes. She didn't just give up; she kept going. So could you. Take Action.
